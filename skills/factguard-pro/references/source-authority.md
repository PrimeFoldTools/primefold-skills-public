# Source Authority Hierarchy

Source credibility varies by domain relevance, verification standards, track record, transparency, and independence.

**Critical Rule**: Even high-authority sources can be wrong. Cross-reference multiple sources.

---

## General Authority Tiers

### Tier 1: Highest Authority
- Peer-reviewed research in top-tier journals
- Government research institutions
- Primary government data
- Audited financial statements
- Court decisions and legal codes

### Tier 2: High Authority
- Peer-reviewed specialized journals
- University research repositories
- Major wire services (AP, Reuters)
- Established papers of record
- Professional analyst reports

### Tier 3: Moderate Authority
- Preprints and working papers
- Trade publications
- Opinion at major outlets
- Conference presentations

### Tier 4: Limited Authority
- Popular science publications
- Emerging digital outlets
- University press releases

### Red Flags: Low/No Authority
- Predatory journals
- Non-peer-reviewed self-published
- Anonymous corporate sources
- Satire/parody sites

---

**Remember**: Authority hierarchies are guidelines, not absolutes. Always verify, especially for important decisions.

# AI Hallucination Patterns Database

Common patterns where AI generates false, plausible-sounding information.

---

## Citation Hallucinations

### Fake Academic Papers
Plausible titles with realistic author names and years that don't exist
**Verification**: Search Google Scholar, check DOI, verify authors

### Fake Statistics
Suspiciously precise numbers without source: "73.2% of remote workers are more productive"
**Verification**: Demand specific citation, check if metric is measurable to that precision

### Fake Book References
Plausible titles and authors for books that don't exist
**Verification**: Check WorldCat, Amazon, Google Books, verify ISBN

---

## Temporal Hallucinations

### Future-as-Past
Treating future events as historical: "The 2026 Olympics were highly successful..."
**Verification**: Check current date vs. event date, search for actual occurrence

### Anachronisms
Technologies placed in wrong time periods: "The 1995 iPhone launch..."
**Verification**: Verify dates via Wikipedia, check technology timeline

---

## Confidence Hallucinations

### Certainty Without Evidence
Definitive claims without support: "All experts agree that..."
**Verification**: Request specific evidence, look for contradictory opinions

### False Consensus
Claiming universal agreement where debate exists
**Verification**: Search for dissenting expert opinions, check scientific literature

---

**Remember:** Multiple hallucination patterns appearing together suggest high confidence in falsehood. Increase scrutiny when patterns compound.
